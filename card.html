Polar bear

Polar bears are the planet's biggest land-based carnivores – although they actually spend most of their lives around water and ice

  • scientific name: Ursus maritimus
  • avarage length: 7 ft 10 in–9 ft 10 in
  • avarage lifespan: 25 years
  • habitat: the Arctic

The most important habitats for polar bears are the edges of pack ice where currents and wind interact, forming a continually melting and refreezing matrix of ice patches and leads (open spaces in the ocean between sea ice).
